Biography

Hui LYU is a Ph.D. candidate in the Graduate School of Life Science at Hokkaido University. Her research focuses on behavioral neurobiology, especially how insects integrate sensory information for spatial perception and navigation.

Using the cricket Gryllus bimaculatus as a model system, she studies antennal mechanosensory processing, sensory-guided behavior, and neural representations of spatial information. Her work combines behavioral experiments, microscopy, electrophysiology, calcium imaging, and computational analysis.

She is currently advised by Prof. Hiroto OGAWA in the Biosystems Science Course at Hokkaido University.

Research Interests

Spatial Perception and Navigation

  • Sensory-guided orientation: Neural and behavioral mechanisms underlying navigation in insects.
  • Spatial representation: How mechanosensory signals are represented in the cricket nervous system.

Antennal Mechanosensory Processing

  • Antennal hair plates: Developmental morphology and three-dimensional sensory structure.
  • Sensorimotor computation: Antennal inputs for contact sensing, body position, and nearby space.

Learning and Behavior

  • Conditioned behavior: Taste aversion and associative learning in the cricket.
  • Behavioral analysis: Quantitative tracking and analysis of insect decision-making.
