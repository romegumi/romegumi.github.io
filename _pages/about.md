    <p class="home-kicker">Behavioral Neurobiology | Insect Sensory Systems</p>
    <h1>Hui LYU</h1>
    <p class="home-lead">
      I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Graduate School of Life Science at Hokkaido University.
      My research focuses on how insects integrate sensory information for spatial perception
      and navigation.
    </p>
    <p>
      Using the cricket <em>Gryllus bimaculatus</em> as a model system, I study antennal
      mechanosensory processing, sensory-guided behavior, and neural representations of spatial
      information. My work combines behavioral experiments, microscopy, electrophysiology,
      calcium imaging, and computational analysis.
    </p>
    <p>
      I am currently advised by Prof.
      <a href="https://researchmap.jp/hogawa">Hiroto OGAWA</a> in the Biosystems Science Course
      at Hokkaido University.
    </p>
  </section>
